My first sketch is of the Clayton house which I had done in 2022. I based this sketch off of a book called Archi doodle. I love sketching houses and the clayton house stood out to me not because of its history but because of the way you could imagine so many different materials when looking at the building in pencil or black and white. Looking at it in black and white it could be made to look modern or traditional, and there are so many different materials you could use on this that would link together quite nicely. Its not until you see this home for what it is that you realise how traditional this home actually is.

This sketch is of the fallingwater house which is a famous building found in america. The building was designed in 1939 by Frank Lloyd Wright and was one of the first homes to blend in with its environment. I love this house not for the way it looks but the way it shows a change in architecture, a movement from traditional architecture to a modern timeline. A way of working with the land around the building to suit it forever. In my career path I wish to work with both land and sustainability and this home is one of the first homes that could possibly hint at both of these. I also completed this sketch in 2022.

The next building is the Heydar Aliyev building which is surprisingly difficult to draw, this building was designed by Zaha Hadid just 3 years before she passed. This building is located in Baku city, Azerbaijan and acts as a Cultural Hub for many things such as a concert hall, library, national museum and more. I wanted to draw this building as it defied what people thought about architecture. It took modernism to a whole new level and I love how the flowing architecture runs across the entire building inside and out. The spacial feeling of this building should remind you how small we are compared to the world around us.

My favourite building to sketch was the Lotus temple, I love this building as it makes architecture a part of nature rather than attempting to blend with it. The point of view you would need as an architect to produce something like this is so vastly different but that is what makes it so incredible. I have 3D printed this structure so I knew I had to create it as a sketch for this project and I love how it turned out. The lotus temple is found in New Delhi India and is considered a place of Worship. Many great architects across the world have visited this building and said great things. This buidling will be an architectural staple of the 20th Century. Forever in Bloom.

The Farnsworth house is another building I drew for this project and its simplicity means little to many eyes across the world, however, this house represented the minimalism era as we know it today. This home was designed in 1950 by Ludwig Mies Van der Rohe, and it showed how we took minimalism in art and carried it into architecture. People before 1950 had never thought of owning a home that looked like this. However, for the farsworths this was all they needed. I didn't draw this building because of how easy it was or how it looked I drew this home because of what it means to architects across the world and how it influences our work.

The last buidling I drew was the Guggenheim Museum. The Guggenheim Museum was also designed by Frank Lloyd Wright and has been a staple point for both art and architecture. I have never drawn anything like the Guggenheim before so this was quite difficult for me to achieve. The Guggenheim is located in New York and was built in 1956. The famous builing competes with the art inside and stands out across the typical architecture found in New York. I love The way it fills the space in such a unique way. The building was originally intended for a man named Solomon Guggenheim to hold his art after Solomon Passed the Guggenheim has since been used to hold art from all across the world. Frank promised that his creation would "mke the building and painting an uninterrupted, beautiful symphony such as never existed in the World of Art before" and he did just that.
