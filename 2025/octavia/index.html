Lamps

Moroccan lamps, crafted from brass, copper, or iron, feature intricate cutwork and coloured glass. When lit, they cast patterned shadows that create warmth and ambience. With domed or lantern-like forms, they blend artistry and function, making them striking accents that bring texture, colour, and atmosphere to traditional or modern interiors.

Lanterns

Moroccan lanterns are handcrafted from metal and glass, featuring intricate patterns that reflect Islamic artistry. When illuminated, they project decorative shadows, adding warmth and atmosphere. Available in hanging, wall-mounted, or freestanding designs, these lanterns combine tradition and functionality, making them timeless decorative elements in both classic and contemporary interiors.

Exteriors

Lush plantings include citrus trees, palms, jasmine, and fragrant herbs, creating a sensory experience through sight, smell, and touch. Colour is introduced through glazed zellige tiles, terracotta pots, and the vivid blooms of bougainvillea, while shaded arcades and carved screens filter light, producing dynamic patterns throughout the day. Moroccan gardens are designed as immersive, layered environments where water, greenery, and built form coexist harmoniously.Today, these principles influence both traditional restorations and contemporary landscape design, where Moroccan gardens continue to represent tranquility, cultural memory, and the timeless pursuit of balance between nature and human habitation.
Moroccan gardens are integral to the country’s cultural and architectural identity, offering spaces of beauty, spirituality, and respite. Rooted in Islamic design principles, these gardens are often enclosed, creating a sense of privacy and sanctuary. The concept of the riad—a courtyard garden within traditional Moroccan homes—epitomises this approach, where nature and architecture are seamlessly integrated. Geometry plays a central role: pathways and planting beds are arranged in symmetrical patterns, often divided into four quadrants by narrow channels of water. Fountains and reflective pools are key elements, not only cooling the air but also symbolising purity and life.
